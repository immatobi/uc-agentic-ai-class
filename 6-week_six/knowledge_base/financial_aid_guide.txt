FINANCIAL AID GUIDE
Academic Year 2026–2027

OVERVIEW

Financial aid helps students finance educational expenses through grants, scholarships, loans, and employment opportunities.

TYPES OF AID

Grants:
Need-based funding that does not require repayment.

Scholarships:
Merit-based or need-based awards.

Student Loans:
Federal and private loan programs.

Work-Study:
Part-time employment opportunities.

APPLICATION PROCESS

Students must submit the Financial Aid Application annually.

PRIORITY DEADLINE

March 1

Applications submitted after March 1 may still be considered but funding may be limited.

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

Students must:
- Be admitted to a degree program.
- Meet satisfactory academic progress requirements.
- Submit required documentation.

SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS

Students must:
- Maintain a GPA of at least 2.0.
- Successfully complete at least 67% of attempted credits.

VERIFICATION

Selected students may be required to submit:
- Tax documents
- Income verification
- Household information

SCHOLARSHIPS

Academic Excellence Scholarship:
GPA 3.8 or higher.

Leadership Scholarship:
Demonstrated leadership and service.

Community Impact Scholarship:
Volunteer and community engagement.

LOANS

Students accepting federal loans must complete entrance counseling.

REFUNDS

Excess financial aid funds are refunded after tuition and fees are paid.

APPEALS

Students who lose eligibility may submit an appeal explaining mitigating circumstances.
