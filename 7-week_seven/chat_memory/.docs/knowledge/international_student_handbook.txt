INTERNATIONAL STUDENT HANDBOOK
Version 2026

WELCOME

The Office of International Student Services supports students from around the world and helps them navigate immigration, academic, and cultural adjustment requirements.

FULL-TIME ENROLLMENT

International students must maintain full-time enrollment.

Undergraduate students:
Minimum 12 credits per semester.

Graduate students:
Minimum 9 credits per semester.

IMMIGRATION DOCUMENTS

Students must maintain valid:

- Passport
- Visa
- I-20 or DS-2019
- I-94 Arrival Record

ADDRESS CHANGES

Students must report address changes within 10 days.

EMPLOYMENT

On-Campus Employment:
Up to 20 hours per week during academic terms.

Off-Campus Employment:
Requires authorization through CPT or OPT programs.

CURRICULAR PRACTICAL TRAINING (CPT)

Students may participate in internships directly related to their major after meeting eligibility requirements.

OPTIONAL PRACTICAL TRAINING (OPT)

OPT permits temporary employment after degree completion.

HEALTH INSURANCE

All international students must maintain approved health insurance coverage throughout enrollment.

ORIENTATION

Attendance at International Student Orientation is mandatory.

ACADEMIC SUCCESS

Students are encouraged to:
- Meet regularly with advisors.
- Use tutoring services.
- Attend workshops.
- Seek support early.

TRAVEL

Students traveling internationally should obtain updated travel signatures before departure.

EMERGENCIES

Students should immediately contact International Student Services if:
- Visa status changes.
- Passport is lost.
- Immigration concerns arise.

CULTURAL ADJUSTMENT

The university offers cultural programming, mentoring, conversation partners, and social events to support integration.
