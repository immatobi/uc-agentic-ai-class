UNIVERSITY STUDENT HANDBOOK

Version 2026

ACADEMIC POLICIES

Students are expected to maintain satisfactory academic progress throughout their studies.

A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 is required for graduation from undergraduate programs.

Students whose GPA falls below 2.0 will be placed on Academic Probation.

Students on Academic Probation for two consecutive semesters may be subject to Academic Suspension.

GRADING SYSTEM

A = 90 - 100
B = 80 - 89
C = 70 - 79
D = 60 - 69
F = Below 60

ATTENDANCE POLICY

Students must attend at least 75% of all scheduled classes.

Students who miss more than 25% of classes without approved justification may be barred from taking final examinations.

Absences due to illness, family emergencies, or university-approved activities may be excused when proper documentation is provided.

EXAMINATION POLICY

Students must present a valid student identification card before entering examination halls.

Cheating, impersonation, or possession of unauthorized materials during examinations constitutes academic misconduct.

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY

Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.

Any student found guilty of plagiarism may receive a failing grade for the affected assignment or course.

Repeated violations may result in suspension or expulsion.

GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS

To graduate, students must:

- Complete all required courses.
- Earn at least 120 credit hours.
- Maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher.
- Satisfy all financial obligations to the university.

STUDENT CONDUCT

Students are expected to demonstrate respect toward faculty, staff, and fellow students.

Harassment, discrimination, violence, or disruptive behavior is prohibited.

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS

Violations of university policies may result in:

- Written warning
- Disciplinary probation
- Suspension
- Expulsion

APPEALS

Students may appeal academic or disciplinary decisions within 14 days of notification.

Appeals must be submitted in writing to the Office of Student Affairs.
